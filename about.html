    • About Me

    I'm Simon Mufara — a computational biologist at the intersection of genomics, web development, and global health equity. I build platforms that make advanced bioinformatics accessible to researchers across Africa and beyond.

    Background

    Simon Mufara is a computational biologist and bioinformatician whose work sits at the crossroads of genomics, software engineering, and health equity. He is currently completing an MSc in Computational Health Informatics at the University of Cape Town's Division of Computational Biology (CBIO), under the supervision of Prof. Nicola Mulder, while simultaneously serving as an NGS Intern at the University of the Free State's Next Generation Sequencing Unit (UFS-NGS) — a WHO Collaborating Centre for vaccine-preventable disease surveillance — under Prof. Martin Nyaga.

    Research & Projects

    His MSc research centres on democratising computational access in African genomics through production-deployed, web-based platforms for single-cell RNA sequencing analysis, variant calling, and pathogen genomic surveillance. Two of these platforms — scrna-analysis.streamlit.app and simon-variants.streamlit.app — are live and actively used.

    Simon holds a BSc Honours in Bioinformatics from UCT and a BHSc from the University of the Witwatersrand. He is a recipient of the DSI-NRF Freestanding Scholarship and was recognised as a Top 10 GradStar Most Employable Graduate in South Africa (2024). His professional experience spans variant calling consultancy at H3D (processing M. tuberculosis clinical isolates), a Global Health Equity Internship at Johnson & Johnson, and a delegate appearance at the PH4GE conference.

    • Leadership & Recognition — Top 10 GradStar Most Employable Graduate (2024), DSI-NRF Scholar, and active contributor to H3ABioNet's pan-African bioinformatics ecosystem.
